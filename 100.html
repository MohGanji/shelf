Do it 100 times first, then we'll talk

It stuck with me when I heard it from MrBeast on Lex Fridman’s podcast.
It’s a simple principle, yet offers a lot of wisdom.

It tells you that you will probably be bad at it the first 99 times, so don’t be hard on yourself.

That you will not see any results at first, so be patient.

It tells you to shut down your analytical mind and just do the damn thing.

To prioritize doing, that you will learn most when you actually do something.

To focus on quantity at the beginning and not on quality.

It is actionable, now you know exactly what to do when you want to start something.

It could be anything you like; painting, blogging, making videos, or learning a new sport.
Whatever it is you want to start, do it 100 times first, then we’ll talk.
